Commercial Sizing & Signals

Scenario-based market sizing (NotebookLM Enterprise, Google Flow) + the internal-signals schema a production version would run on
TAM — Total Addressable Market
The total annual revenue opportunity if a product captured 100% of the market it can serve. Always an upper bound, never a forecast. Two independent estimation methods below; the defensible answer is where they overlap.
Top-down formula
Start from a published category size, narrow to the addressable part. Two variants depending on whether the category is legacy spend being converted to AI, or already AI-native: TAM = Legacy_category_$ × AI_conversion_rate TAM = AI_native_category_$ × addressable_segment_share
Bottom-up formula
Start from the people who would use the product, multiply up: TAM = Seat_population × Attach_rate × Price_per_seat_annual
Attach rate
The % of an addressable population that converts to paid seats. No public source provides this for any specific product — it is always an assumption. Here it is a declared Scenario parameter (1% / 5% / 10%), never presented as data.
AI conversion rate
The % of legacy-category spend or firms converting to AI tools. Published measurements disagree because their bars differ: Census BTOS 19.8% of all US firms / 37% of 250+-employee firms use AI in any function (May 2026); a16z 29% of the Fortune 500 are live, paying enterprise-AI customers (Apr 2026). Range them; never average them.
Triangulation rule
Run top-down and bottom-up independently. Overlap = defensible band. A large gap is diagnostic, not noise: it means the category boundary is too broad, the attach assumption is miscalibrated, or the seat population is too narrow. Fix the assumption the gap points to — don't split the difference.
Confidence tiers used below
Confirmed verified by direct fetch of the primary source, dated  ·  Tracker third-party figure, not vendor-published  ·  Unverified carried from earlier research, not independently re-fetched  ·  Scenario declared assumption, user-selected, not data.
Standing caveats (apply to everything below)
Seat populations are US-only, formal-job-title-only (BLS) — a floor, not a ceiling. Free-tier usage is invisible to spend data. Bundled products (NotebookLM/Flow ship inside Workspace and AI subscriptions) are undercounted by procurement panels. Sizes the paid opportunity only.

Prototype scope & limitations — read before evaluating

1. Detection is manual in this prototype. The "change detected" flag records a change hand-sourced from the vendor's own documentation; no crawler or diffing pipeline runs behind it. The prototype demonstrates the post-detection workflow — diff, urgency flag, affected products, documented upsell path, memo. The production design is a watcher over a registry of tracked assets, diffing snapshots and routing structured alerts, with announcement lead time (here, 27 days) as the key signal.

2. Utilization is not measured. Asset health is freshness and utilization; utilization requires internal instrumentation (asset views/shares, field pulls, opportunity attach rate) unavailable from public data. It is shown as N/A rather than fabricated. Freshness thresholds (30/60 days) are an illustrative rubric; a production version replaces calendar age with event-driven staleness tied to dependency changes.

3. The memo generator sequences; it does not author. Actions are drawn verbatim from the underlying strategy analysis. Asset and competitor selection reflects that analysis's target gap; a production scorecard would run on a jointly owned asset registry, not a hand-picked list.
